Neo-Versailles

Level Designer - Scripting - Build Engineer

Duration: August 2022 - May 2023

Neo-Versailles is a Unity First-Person Horror game collaboration between ArtCenter College of Design and Rochester Institute of Technology.


I worked on this project as a level designer and systems programmer. I developed our saving and loading system and worked on blocking out the final level of the game.


Our world is set in the world of Neo-Versailles, an alternative version of Versailles, France after the conclusion of the first French Revolution. Neo-Versailles was once hailed as the artistic and technological hub of the world, up until the Great Burning of Neo-Versailles, in which the Art-Breakers under the orders of the monarchy burn the art that dwells in Neo-Versailles. This act results in the escape of Demons of Despair, monstrous demons hidden inside of art, who roam rampant in Neo-Versailles, wreaking havoc and bringing despair to the once lively city. The only ones who can stop them are the order of the Art-Keepers, a secret society of artists dating back to the beginning of time, who devote their lives to the conduct of art exorcism, and are the only ones blessed with the knowledge and training of art-keeping, the act used in order to trap Demons of Despair back into their artistic vessels, turning haunting and terrifying figures into beautiful art.



Where Does This Ocean Go?

Level Designer - Narrative Designer

Duration: 1 Month

This project was an exercise in making a narrative within a game world with kitbash pieces in Unreal 4 by adapting a music piece from another medium


The goal was to take a song, "where does this ocean go?" by Ilaria Graziano from the Ghost in the Shell: Stand Alone Complex animated series, and craft my own Sci-fi narrative using the song.


I used the Paragon: Lt. Belica package by Epic Games, City Subway Train Modular package by Dekogon Studios, and Water Materials package by tharlevfx from the Unreal Engine Market place to tell a story of police officer in a Sci-fi future that looks back through her memories on a surreal train. She remembers getting injured on duty and being repair with cybernetic enhancements. Now that she is a cyborg she feels trapped in her metal prison distant from the world wonders where the ocean goes.

Horror Level

Level Designer - Narrative Designer - Scripting

Duration: 2 Weeks

This project was focused on using spatial and enviromental storytelling to create a horror walking simulator experience in Unreal 4


I utilized assets from the Unreal Engine Market Place to create a horror narrative where the player slowly walks through a park losing touch with reality. They see a combination of blood, gore, and food slowly slipping more out of reality until they wind up mysteriously in a dining room where gore and human remains are set up for a feast.


Who put together this feast and killed those people? Was it you?
